# Market Trends

> Macro shifts worth watching. Anything large enough to influence strategy or hypothesis design.

## Active trends

**1. AI-augmented incident response (AIOps)**
Competitors (PagerDuty, Datadog, Opsgenie) are investing in AI-powered noise reduction, incident correlation, and auto-remediation. Engineering teams increasingly expect AI assistance during incidents. This trend directly validates the AI Incident Copilot and predictive-incident-prevention bets.
- First observed: 2026-07-11 (init)
- Strategic relevance: high — Pulse's AI roadmap is the right category bet; execution speed matters

**2. Slack-native incident management**
incident.io competes primarily by being Slack-native. Engineering teams that "live in Slack" may prefer lighter-touch tools. slack-v3-integration is a direct response to this threat.
- First observed: 2026-07-11 (init)
- Strategic relevance: high — must make Pulse a first-class Slack participant to compete

**3. Enterprise consolidation**
Enterprise customers consolidating tooling stacks (fewer, deeper tools). Works in Pulse's favor if it can prove org-wide value post-pilot, but also means PagerDuty/Atlassian bundling is a real threat.
- First observed: 2026-07-11 (init)
- Strategic relevance: high — directly tied to enterprise adoption priority

**4. CNCF / cloud-native ecosystem maturity**
Kubernetes, service mesh, and cloud-native patterns are mainstream. Engineering teams expect incident tooling to be ecosystem-aware (service dependencies, deployment signals). Relevant to predictive-incident-prevention.
- First observed: 2026-07-11 (init)
- Strategic relevance: medium — shapes what "infrastructure metrics" means for predictive features
