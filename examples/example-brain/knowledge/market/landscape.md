# Market Landscape

> Category map. Who plays where. Where this product sits. Refresh quarterly or when a meaningful new entrant shows up.

## Category definition

**Incident Management & Operational Intelligence** — tools that help engineering teams detect, respond to, communicate about, and learn from software incidents. Expanding to include AI-assisted operations (AIOps).

## Players

### Direct competitors

| Company | Product | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| PagerDuty | Incident management, on-call scheduling, AIOps | Market leader; investing heavily in AIOps and enterprise features |
| Atlassian | Jira Service Management | Bundled with Jira ecosystem; strong enterprise footprint |
| Opsgenie | On-call and alerting (Atlassian) | Merged into Jira SM; legacy customer base |
| FireHydrant | Incident management and postmortems | Close direct competitor; similar positioning to Pulse |
| incident.io | Incident management (Slack-native) | Strong Slack integration; competes on simplicity and speed |

### Adjacent

- StatusPage / Atlassian Statuspage — customer communication during incidents
- Grafana (observability) — overlaps with analytics use case
- Datadog (observability + incident management) — expanding into incident workflow

### Platforms / dependencies

- Slack — Pulse deeply integrated; also how incident.io competes
- PagerDuty — alerting dependency for many customers
- GitHub, Jira — engineering workflow integrations

### Replacements (non-software)

- Spreadsheets / Google Docs for postmortems
- Slack channels alone (no dedicated incident tooling)
- Custom internal tooling

## Positioning

Pulse sits as a **collaborative incident workspace** for remote engineering teams — more opinionated than Jira SM, more collaborative than PagerDuty, more analytically capable than incident.io, with a differentiated AI roadmap (Copilot, predictive prevention).
